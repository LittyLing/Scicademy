Submit Your Research

  • Must be at least 40 characters

  • Make sure your paper is in PDF format. Either save it as a pdf, or use a free online pdf converter

  • An introduction usually describes the theoretical background, indicates why the work is important, states a specific research question, and poses a specific hypothesis to be tested.

  • The methods section describes both specific techniques and the overall experimental strategy used by the scientists.

  • The purpose of a results section is to present and illustrate your findings. Make this section a completely objective report of the results, and save all interpretation for the discussion.

  • The objective here is to provide an interpretation of your results and support for all of your conclusions, using evidence from your experiment and generally accepted knowledge, if appropriate. The significance of findings should be clearly described.

  • The acknowledgments tell you what people or institutions (besides the authors) contributed to the work.

  • This section provides the sources cited throughout the paper. Need help creating citations? http://www.citationmachine.net/apa/cite-a-journal Try to avoid using websites and instead use primary sources (such as scientific papers in journals).
